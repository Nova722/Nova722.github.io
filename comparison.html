Mass shootings increased from 2014-2016 and decreased from 2016-2017. However, the number of victims of mass shootings increased from 2016-2017 indicating mass shootings that year were more deadly.

When comparing mass shooting counts states with large urban centers such as California, Illinois and Florida have the most mass shootings. When taking population into account we can see that the per capita mass shooting visualization paints a different picture with many states in the south such as Louisiana, Tennessee and Missiouri come out with higher mass shooting rates per capita.

The number of hate groups has consistently increased from 2014 - 2017. There is a positive linear correlation between States with 1 or more mass shooting and the number hate groups in a given state as seen from our regression.
